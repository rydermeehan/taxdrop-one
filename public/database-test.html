How We Calculate Your Savings Estimate

▾

Our engine uses the same methodology that professional property tax consultants and appraisal districts use. Here's the 6-step process, powered by real ATTOM property data.

1

Pull Subject Property Data

We fetch your property's assessed value, square footage, year built, beds/baths, lot size, garage, and pool from ATTOM's expanded profile. This is the official data appraisal districts use.

2

Find & Filter Comparable Properties

We search nearby properties using an adaptive radius (tighter in dense urban areas, wider in suburban). We filter out comps with wildly different sizes, nominal transfers ($0 sales), sales older than 24 months, and wrong property types. In California, we also filter out extremely low Prop 13 base-year assessments.

3

Adjust Each Comp for Differences

Every comp is adjusted to be "apples to apples" with your property. A smaller comp has inflated $/sqft — we adjust it down. A newer comp sells higher — we adjust for that. We also adjust for bedroom/bathroom count, lot size differences, and weight closer comps more heavily.

4

Calculate the Market Baseline

We take the weighted 40th percentile of all adjusted comp values. This is a standard protest strategy — the 40th percentile is defensible evidence that the property is worth less than the district's assessment, while the 50th (median) would be "fair market."

5

Apply Depreciation & Amenity Discounts

Older buildings get an age depreciation discount (2-8% based on age brackets, capped at 15%). If your property lacks a garage or pool, we apply an additional discount since the comp pool likely includes properties with those amenities inflating market values.

6

Calculate Gap & Savings

We compare your property's current $/sqft to the adjusted baseline. The gap shows how much you're over-assessed. We multiply the baseline by your square footage, round down to the nearest $5,000, and calculate annual savings using your county's actual tax rate (from ATTOM data, not state averages).

Comp Adjustments We Make

  • GLA (size): 10% size diff = 1% $/sqft adjustment
  • Year built: 0.3% per year difference (max 15%)
  • Bedrooms: $7,500 per bedroom difference
  • Bathrooms: $12,500 per bathroom difference
  • Lot size: $3/sqft of lot difference (max 10%)
  • Distance: Exponential decay weighting (closer = more weight)

Confidence Levels

  • STRONG: 5%+ gap with 3+ comps
  • MODERATE: 3%+ gap with 2+ comps
  • MARGINAL: 1%+ gap with 1+ comp
  • NO_CASE: Property assessed at or below market

Data Sources

  • Sales comps: Recent nearby sales (ATTOM)
  • Equity comps: Nearby assessed values (ATTOM)
  • Tax rate: Actual county rate from ATTOM tax records
