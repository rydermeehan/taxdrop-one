Frequently Asked Questions

Can my taxes go up if I protest? ▼

No. In Texas, protesting your appraised value cannot result in a higher value for that tax year. In California, filing an assessment appeal also protects you from an increase during the appeal period. There is zero risk to filing.

Do I need to attend a hearing in person? ▼

In Texas, most counties now allow phone hearings or online submissions. Many protests are resolved at the informal hearing stage without needing a formal ARB hearing. In California, some counties offer hearings by phone or mail. Your county filing guide will have the specific options available.

How do you find the comparable properties? ▼

We use ATTOM property data, the same data source used by appraisal districts, lenders, and real estate professionals. We search nearby properties with similar characteristics, filter out outliers and non-arms-length transactions, and adjust each comp for differences in size, age, bedrooms, bathrooms, and lot size.

What if my estimated savings are low? ▼

Even modest savings compound over time. A $500/year reduction saves you $2,500 over five years. The $79 package cost is a one-time fee and the evidence documents can be reused or updated for subsequent years. If our analysis shows your property is fairly assessed, we will tell you that upfront.
