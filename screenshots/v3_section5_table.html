AdjustmentMagnitudeWhat it corrects for
Size (GLA) ±10% per size delta Economies of scale: bigger homes have lower $/sqft (fixed costs — kitchen, roof, mechanicals — amortize over more square footage). Larger comp adjusts up toward the subject's smaller footprint; smaller comp adjusts down.
Age 0.3%/yr · year delta capped at ±15 yr (max ±4.5%) Newer comp adjusts down; older comp adjusts up. Uses effective year built when the CAD publishes it — a renovated 1967 home with an effective year of 2007 compares as a 2007 build, not a 1967 build.
Land value max ±10% of $/sqft Adjusts for CAD-published land valuation differences between the subject and comp. The cap prevents a single high-land-value comp from collapsing the baseline on a low-land subject (or vice versa).
Additional (pools, garages, misc.) flat-$ per CAD-published methodology Pool, garage spaces, fireplace, and similar features are adjusted in dollar terms when the CAD publishes the flag. Values match HCAD/DCAD published methodology. Skipped on individual comps when the relevant flag is missing.
Single-comp cap ±30% from raw No single comp can adjust more than 30% from its unadjusted $/sqft. Keeps the brief defensible against a CAD reviewer who would challenge a 50% swing on any one comparable.
